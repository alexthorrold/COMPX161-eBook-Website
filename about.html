Synopsis

The quintessential detective Sherlock Holmes and his partner, Dr. Watson make their debut in Arthur Conan Doyle's "The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes". This book contains a collection of Holmes and Watson's first 12 adventures, first published on 14th October 1892. The stories follow the pair in their attempts to unravel various mysteries, told from the eyes of Dr. Watson.

About the author

The man behind Sherlock Holmes - British writer and physician Sir Arthur Conan Doyle. Like many prolific writers, he struggled to find a publisher early in his career. He wrote not just stories about Sherlock and Watson, but also many other plays, romances, non-fictions, and the like. He was knighted for his work in a field hospital during the Boer War.
